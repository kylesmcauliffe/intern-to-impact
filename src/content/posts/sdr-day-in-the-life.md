## Why day-to-day matters more than culture tours

Company visits are great for vibe — but I wish someone had walked me through **what people actually do** in their first role. That clarity changed how I applied and interviewed.

## Sales hierarchy (simplified)

1. **SDR / BDR** — First touch. Qualify interest, book meetings, learn the product.
2. **Account Executive** — Closes deals. Owns the relationship deeper in the cycle.
3. **Enterprise vs SMB** — Deal size and complexity differ; paths vary by company.

## My day as an SDR (high level)

- Review **inbound leads** — people who already showed interest
- **Discovery calls** — understand their needs, explain how we help
- **CRM updates** — log conversations so the team stays aligned
- **Collaboration** — work with account executives when a lead is ready

## Internship vs full-time

My Uber Eats internship was **full-cycle sales** — pitch to close. My current SDR role is **first touch** on inbound leads. Both taught me to communicate clearly and handle rejection.

## If you're exploring sales

Ask people: *What percentage of your day is calls vs email vs research?* *Inbound or outbound?* That tells you more than a mission statement.
